BUBBLE TEA

Bubble tea (also known as pearl milk tea, bubble milk tea, tapioca milk tea, or boba tea or boba; Chinese: 珍珠奶茶; pinyin: zhēnzhū nǎichá, 波霸奶茶; bōbà nǎichá; 泡泡茶; pàopào chá) is a tea-based drink that originated in Taiwan in the early 1980s.It most commonly consists of tea accompanied by chewy tapioca balls ("boba" or "pearls"), but it can be made with other toppings as well.

INGREDIENTS

Hot brewed tea
  • 2 ¼ cups water heated to 205°F / 96°C (preferably filtered)
  • 2 ½ tbsp black tea I used ceylon OP tea. Other options are Assam tea, or Chinese breakfast tea.
Boba pearls
  • 2 ½ cups water at room temperature (preferably filtered)
  • 15 tbsp black tea
Sugar syrup
  • ½ cup brown sugar or white sugar
  • ¼ cup water

INSTRUCTIONS

  1. the tea leaves in a brewing pot or French press.
  2. Heat the water to 205°F / 96°C (if you don't have a thermometer to measure the temperature, bring the water to a boil, and then leave it off-heat for about 3 minutes to reach the correct temp.), and THEN measure 2 ¼ cup of water into the brewing pot. Stir once or twice to make sure the tea leaves are saturated with water, then cover the pot and let it sit for no more than 5 - 6 minutes.
  3. Strain the tea leaves, and place the tea in the fridge to cool down.
  4. In a glass (15 - 16 oz), place some boba pearls, along with some of the sugar syrup.
  5. Pour about 1 cup of brewed tea. The tea should be chilled or at least at room temperature (if you’re adding ice).
  6. Add ice, and top up the glass with milk. Stir to combine. Taste and add more sugar syrup if you'd like the drink sweeter.
